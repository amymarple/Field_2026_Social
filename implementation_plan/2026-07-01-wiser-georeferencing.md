# Implementation plan — WISER frame → physical paddock georeferencing

**Date:** 2026-07-01
**Status:** tooling implemented; awaiting field survey to produce a *confirmed* transform.

## Goal and motivation

Tie the WISER UWB tracking frame (native inches, offset origin) to the surveyed physical
paddock frame the CV pipeline already uses, so spatial WISER results stop carrying the
"coordinate frame unverified" caveat and can be cross-validated against the cameras.

## Current problem (concrete)

- `data_manifests/2026-06-29-wiser-pilot.yaml` records `georeferenced_to_paddock: false`; the
  WISER x,y have an unknown offset origin and unknown orientation/scale relative to the paddock.
- `wiser_tracking_analysis/ANALYSIS_STATUS.md` lists this as the #1 (P0) blocker: route
  structure, thigmotaxis, and route-vs-boundary claims may be coordinate artifacts, and the
  provisional boundary comes from `observed_extent` (the data's own bounding box), not the real
  paddock walls.
- The only "ground truth" today (`configs/fixed_position_ground_truth.csv`) is the *median of a
  tag's own estimates* — self-referential, so it cannot anchor an absolute frame.

## Why now

It gates the publishability of every spatial candidate finding (per the status tracker), and it
is a prerequisite for WISER×CV cross-validation. Building the tooling now lets the field survey
be run and consumed immediately.

## Governing constraint — WISER is noisy

~7 in median jitter (p95 ~15 in), ~2% impossible-jump/dropout fixes, and worst accuracy at the
paddock edges/corners (geometric dilution). The transform fixes only the **frame** (origin,
rotation, scale); it cannot reduce per-fix noise, so downstream spatial claims stay gated by the
~7 in floor. Fit residuals will bottom out at the jitter floor (~18 cm), not zero.

## Target frame (reused, not redefined)

`preprocessing/computer_vision/field_coords.py` + `configs/field_layout.json`: origin at pole
**A0 = (0,0)**, **x = 1219.2 cm** (40 ft length), **y = 609.6 cm** (20 ft width), cm units,
15-pole grid A0–C4 with surveyed coordinates, `IN_TO_CM = 2.54`. `resolve_landmark(name, layout)`
maps a pole/marker/shelter-corner name to field cm.

## Inputs / outputs

- **Input (field):** `wiser_georef_survey.csv` — one row per surveyed pole dwell:
  `label` (pole/landmark name), `shortid` (tag used), `start_local`/`end_local` (dwell window,
  local EDT), and optional manual `wiser_x`/`wiser_y` (inches) fallback. `#`-prefixed lines and
  blanks are ignored.
- **Input (data):** the WISER SQLite DB (read-only) to extract dwell medians when manual xy are
  not given.
- **Output:** `wiser_to_field_transform.json` (matrix, scale, rotation, per-point residuals,
  correspondences, `confirmed`) + a validation overlay PNG under `outputs/` (git-ignored).

## Transform math

**2D similarity via Umeyama (1991)** — rotation + uniform scale + translation (4 DoF), the
physically correct model for two metric frames, reflection-free. Represented as a 2×3 affine
`[[a,b,tx],[c,d,ty]]` mapping WISER-inch (x,y) → field-cm. `scale = √(a²+c²)` must land near
2.54 cm/in (built-in sanity check); `rotation = atan2(c,a)`.

**Robust fit:** iteratively fit, compute per-point reprojection residual, drop points whose
residual exceeds `max(k·MAD, floor)` (default k=3, floor tied to the jitter floor), refit; report
which points were dropped. A full **affine** (6 DoF, lstsq) is fit as a *diagnostic only* to
report shear/anisotropy (should be negligible; large values mean a bad survey, not real shear).

## Timestamp / coordinate assumptions

- WISER timestamps are Unix **ms UTC**; survey dwell windows are entered in **local EDT (−04:00)**
  and converted to UTC ms before the bounded read (reusing `time_utils`/`_roi_time_utc` patterns).
- Source frame: WISER native inches, offset origin. Target frame: field cm, origin A0.
- Per-dwell reduction: validity-filter (`add_speed` + `add_validity_flags`, keep `valid`), then
  **median** x,y with the per-dwell radial scatter (std, p95, n) recorded for QC.

## Expected behavior

- With no confirmed transform, `load_field_transform()` returns `None` and every analysis behaves
  exactly as today (transform is a strict no-op) — the config ships `confirmed: false`.
- Once a survey is fit and QC passes, `verified_boundary_in_wiser()` inverse-maps the A0–C4
  rectangle into WISER inches to serve as a *confirmed* boundary (replacing `observed_extent`) for
  `distance_to_edge`/`thigmotaxis_index`/route boundary, and `apply_field_transform()` adds
  `x_field_cm,y_field_cm` for CV cross-checks. Edge/thigmotaxis bands stay ≥ the jitter floor.

## Verification

- `python scripts/selftest_georeference.py` — (a) noise-free round-trip recovers a known
  similarity exactly (rmse≈0, scale/rotation recovered); (b) noisy case (~7 in jitter + one gross
  outlier) recovers scale within tolerance, residuals ≈ injected noise, outlier flagged; PASS.
- With a real survey: per-point residual within each pole's own dwell scatter (≈7 in / 18 cm
  floor, not ~0); scale within a few % of 2.54 cm/in; negligible affine shear; no single point
  dominates; overlay shows the WISER cloud inside the 20×40 ft rectangle with poles aligning.
- Non-regression: with `confirmed: false`, an existing driver (e.g. `analyze_route_structure.py`)
  produces byte-identical output.

## Non-goals

- Not re-expressing the whole pipeline in cm (inch thresholds — 7 in jitter, 12.5 in/s speed,
  inch ROIs — stay valid); field-cm is additive. Full cm migration is future work.
- Not reducing WISER per-fix noise; georeferencing fixes the frame only.
- Not auto-editing every analysis driver; drivers opt in to the verified boundary via the new
  helpers.
